Good messages tell users what happened, why it matters, and what to do next. Every message in the Shopware Admin should be clear, honest, and respectful of the user's time.

## Error messages

Error messages appear after something has gone wrong. Tell users what failed and how to recover, not to apologise or assign blame. Lead with the most likely fix. Avoid "sorry", "please", and softeners like "oops": they trivialise real problems and undermine trust.

<DoDont
  do="Product could not be saved. Check your connection and try again."
  dont="Error: An error occurred while attempting to save the product. Please try again."
/>

<DoDont
  do="Import failed. The file format is not supported. Use CSV or XLSX."
  dont="Something went wrong with your import."
/>

## Success messages

Success messages confirm that an action completed. Confirm the outcome and get out of the way. Avoid exclamation marks and "Success!" as a label. Reserve stronger celebration for significant milestones, not routine actions.

<DoDont
  do="Product saved."
  dont="Success! The product has been successfully saved."
/>

<DoDont
  do="Order cancelled. The customer will be refunded within 5 business days."
  dont="Your order has been cancelled successfully."
/>

## Warning messages

Warning messages appear before a risky or irreversible action. They prepare users, not punish them. Be calm and informative. Use "we" instead of "you" when describing issues caused by the system rather than the user.

Three common patterns:

- **Raise awareness:** alert users to a condition that may affect them, without requiring immediate action
- **Highlight an imminent problem:** prompt action with a clear timeline or consequence
- **Confirm a risky action:** state what will happen and whether it can be undone

<DoDont
  do="Unsaved changes will be lost. Leave the page or save your changes first."
  dont="Warning: You have unsaved changes!"
/>

<DoDont
  do="Deleting this product will remove it from all associated sales channels."
  dont="Are you sure you want to delete this product?"
/>

## Info messages

Info messages provide context that helps users understand the current state. They do not require action and should not interrupt the workflow. Get to the point and stop: do not pad with filler phrases.

<DoDont
  do="Prices are shown excluding VAT. Switch to gross display in your settings."
  dont="Please note that for your information, prices on this page are currently being displayed without VAT applied."
/>

## Empty states

Use the `mt-empty-state` component for empty states wherever possible.

Empty states appear when a list, table, or section has no content yet. There are two distinct situations:

- **Blank slate:** the user has not yet added anything. Introduce the feature and prompt the first action.
- **Cleared state:** the user completed a task or filtered out all results. Acknowledge the outcome and suggest what to do next.

Write the heading as a direct observation, not an apology. Tell users what they can do from here.

<DoDont
  do="No products yet. Add your first product to start selling."
  dont="Unfortunately, there are no products to display at this time."
/>

<DoDont
  do="No results for 'blue shirt'. Try a different search term or clear your filters."
  dont="Your search returned no results."
/>

## Loading states

Loading messages should be brief. The user already knows the app is working.

Use a label only when it adds context about what is happening. For generic operations, no label is better than a vague one.

<DoDont
  do="Loading products..."
  dont="Please wait while we load your data. This may take a moment."
/>

<DoDont do="Importing orders..." dont="Loading..." />
